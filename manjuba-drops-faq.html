Safety and Side Effects

Is Manjuba Drops safe?

The formula uses natural ingredients at doses consistent with clinical research. It is manufactured in an FDA-registered, GMP-certified facility in the USA and does not contain synthetic hormones, artificial stimulants, or pharmaceutical compounds. That said, "safe" depends on your individual health situation. If you have a medical condition or take prescription medication, talk to your doctor before starting any new supplement.

Are there any side effects?

Most men tolerate Manjuba Drops well with no issues. Some may notice mild digestive discomfort, changes in appetite, or slightly more frequent urination during the first few days as the body adjusts. These effects typically resolve on their own within a week. If you experience anything unusual or persistent, stop taking the product and consult your healthcare provider.

Can I take Manjuba Drops with other medications?

Some ingredients in Manjuba Drops may interact with certain prescription medications. Epimedium can affect blood pressure and blood clotting. Saw palmetto may interact with hormone therapies. Tribulus and fenugreek can influence hormone levels. If you take blood thinners, blood pressure medication, nitrates, or hormone-related drugs, consult your doctor before using this product.

Who should NOT take Manjuba Drops?

Manjuba Drops is designed for adult men over 40. It is not intended for women, children, or men under 18. If you have a hormone-sensitive condition (such as prostate cancer or breast cancer), do not use this product without explicit approval from your physician. Pregnant or nursing women should avoid it entirely.
